# Feature Dictionary

The final clustering model was trained using a subset of engineered features designed to capture different dimensions of market behavior, including efficiency, liquidity, volatility, persistence, activity, and order-flow dynamics.

| Feature            | Category             | Description                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| ------------------ | -------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| rolling_imbalance  | Order Flow           | Measures the imbalance between buying and selling volume over a rolling window. Positive values indicate dominant buying pressure, while negative values indicate dominant selling pressure. |
| efficiency_ratio   | Market Efficiency    | Quantifies how efficiently price moves from one point to another by comparing net directional movement to total price noise. Higher values indicate more directional behavior.               |
| compression        | Volatility Structure | Measures volatility contraction by calculating the rolling standard deviation of the trading range. Lower values may indicate compressed market conditions preceding expansion.              |
| amihud             | Liquidity            | Amihud illiquidity measure. Estimates the price impact generated by traded volume. Higher values suggest lower liquidity and greater market impact per unit of volume.                       |
| persistence        | Trend Persistence    | Measures the tendency of consecutive returns to maintain the same direction. Higher values indicate stronger trend continuation behavior.                                                    |
| volume_range_ratio | Market Activity      | Relates trading volume to price range, providing a proxy for trading intensity relative to market movement.                                                                                  |
| volatility         | Volatility           | Rolling standard deviation of log returns used to quantify short-term market variability and uncertainty.                                                                                    |

## Feature Categories

The selected variables were chosen to represent multiple dimensions of market structure:

* **Order Flow:** buying and selling pressure.
* **Market Efficiency:** directional versus noisy behavior.
* **Liquidity:** transaction impact and market depth proxies.
* **Persistence:** trend continuation characteristics.
* **Activity:** trading intensity.
* **Volatility:** market uncertainty and compression/expansion dynamics.

These features form the input space used by the clustering algorithm to identify market regimes.
